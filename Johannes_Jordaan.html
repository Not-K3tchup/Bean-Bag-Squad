Internet Protocol

What is the Internet Protocol?

The Internet Protocol Suite (commonly referred to as TCP/IP) is the primary set (or communications protocol) of digital message formats and rules for transferring messages between computers across a single network or a series of interconnected networks. Datagrams, often known as data packets or simply packets, are used to send and receive messages (techopedia, 2019)

In networking, a protocol is a standardized manner of doing sure movements and formatting information in order that or extra devices are capable of talk with and recognize every other (Library Learning, n.d.).

Almost always, the package (datagram) is separated into pieces, but each piece carries the receiver's address (destination host). Each component eventually reaches the intended recipient, frequently via different routes and at different times. The Postal System, which is the IP, also determines these routes and times (Monteith, 2018). The Postal System, on the other hand, reassembles all the components before delivering them to the recipient (at the transport and application levels) (destination host) (techopedia, 2019).

IP is a connectionless protocol, which means that no connection to the receiver (destination host) is required before transmission (by the source host), continuing the analogy, before the letter/package is shipped, there does not need to be a direct connection between the physical return address on the letter/package and the receiving address (techopedia, 2019).
