World Wide Web vs The Internet


The differences between the World Wide Web and the Internet


To someone with little to no knowledge of the internet and the world wide web, it is easy for them to think that both these vastly different things are one in the same. The terms 'internet' as well as 'World Wide Web' may at first glance seem interchangeable, but when diving deeper into the topic, it becomes much clearer that these two terms are vastly different from each other.


Defining the relationship

What is the world wide web?


The World Wide Web can be defined as a network of web pages that are found through their web-address. The idea of the web started out as a proposal from Sir Tim Berners-Lee at CERN in Europe. The software that was developed around Berbers-Lee concept became a part of a public domain on which companies and individuals alike could create their own websites.


What is the internet?


The internet is a network that is built out of a global connection of computers that communicate with eaach other. This network of computers act as the base on which the world wide web is built and is the basis for each and every type of digitally transfered data. The web is the main way of having a connection to the internet. When sending an e-mail or a message, the data gets transfered through the internet to the intended destination


Summary


To sum up and to come to a conclusion. When considering the definitions for both the World Wide Web and the Internet, we can see that the difference that seperates the two from one another are prevelant. Both of them work together to be able to function accordngly. Although the web is much more dependant on having the internet, than the internet is on having the web, it is still important to have both work together.
